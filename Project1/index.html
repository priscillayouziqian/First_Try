About Iceland

The capital Reykjavik is one of the cleanest places you will ever visit. But ironically, translated it means “Smoky Bay”.

Because smoke, or rather steam, was the first thing the city founder Ingolfur Arnarson saw rising from the thermal hot springs in the 9th century. Today, this steam is used for heating up the whole city of Reykjavik.

Iceland is a volcanic island, which originated only about 16 million years ago and is thus one of Europe’s youngest regions.The island's landscape really is unique, with its fjords, impressive waterfalls, bubbling geysers and gigantic glaciers as well as the wild, untouched highlands, which often resemble a lunar landscape.
